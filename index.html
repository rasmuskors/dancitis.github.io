What is Dancitis?

Dan..Dance...Dancitis? A physical state which causes the individual to experience the uncontrollable need to dance. Dancitis can strike anyone at any time regardless of dance experience or talent and is not limited to occasions when music is present. I got Dancitis ! And I bet you want to get it too!

SYMPTOMS
  • Feeling happy to learn a new skill while having lots of fun
  • Feeling smart because dancing improves your mental sharpness, agility and memory.
  • You get better in communication skills, passion, perseverance and creativity
  • You start building lasting memories and friendships
  • You get bursts of dopamine, serotonin, endorphines and oxytocin which are the positive neurochemicals that make you happy in every sense
DIAGNOSIS
  • When listening to music experience the uncontrollable need to dance.
CAUSES
  • It can be genetically inherited from the artistic vein of someone in the family.

SALSA

Salsa means sauce in Spanish and it´s as well a form of Latin dance! Cubans, Puerto Ricans and Colombians who were living in New York are credited as the pioneers of Salsa music - a music that grew out of Mambo and Cuban Son roots. During the 1960s, Mambo dancing was already very popular in New York so as salsa dance developed, one of the basic steps in Salsa dancing is the Mambo basic. Salsa then became popular in Latinamerica specially Puerto Rico, Cuba and Colombia and each of these countries developed its own brand of Salsa music and dancing. Simultaneously, the popularity of Salsa music and dance spread across North America which developed its own brand of Salsa dancing as well. Therefore we have different ways of dancing to the same salsa music. The most popular ones are L.A. (On1), N.Y. (On2), Cuban and Colombian.

BACHATA

Bachata is a dance from the Dominican Republic in the Caribbean islands. Both the music and the dance have been influenced by Merengue, Cuban bolero, Salsa and Cumbia. The music is romantic and often telling the story of a man and women in love. The music has evolved rapidly from traditional Bachata in the first half of the 20th Century to modern Bachata and the creation of different urban Bachata Styles which makes it as popular as Salsa nowadays. As the music evolves the dancing styles evolved too from the traditional style which was more like a bolero developed into a modern Bachata with more turns and arms variations and then to what is called nowadays “Dominican Style” which is characterized for having rapid footwork and a more happy and spicy mood and the Sensual Bachata style which was created in Spain and its characterized for the body rolls and the romantic mood.

ZUMBA

Zumba is a dance fitness program created by Colombian dancer and choreographer Alberto "Beto" Perez during the 1990s. Zumba involves dance and aerobic movements performed to energetic music. The choreography incorporates Hip-hop, Soca, Samba, Salsa, Merengue, Reggaeton, Belly dance, Flamenco, Cumbia, Quebradita and squats and lunges are also included. Approximately 15 million people take weekly Zumba classes in over 200,000 locations across 180 countries. It´s perfect for getting fit and having fun !
